About Tantra Authority

Tantra Authority is published by The Naked Press, the home of Lawrence Gregory's body of work. We exist to lay out a particular tantric approach — body-grounded, anti-mythology, and unusual in that it treats the dismantling work as central to the practice itself, rather than as preparation for it.

What this approach adds (and what most tantric education leaves out)

Most modern tantric writing focuses on the body practices — breath, sound, movement, touch, attention. Those are real. But on their own they produce frustrated practitioners going through motions, because the practices land in bodies still running inherited beliefs, traumas, training, ideologies, ideals, and embedded patterns that block them.

The approach this site documents has two movements happening at the same time:

What you do — the body practices: breath, sound, vocalization, singing, dance, movement, touch, chant, focused attention, and explicit energy work. Not "energy" as metaphysical decoration — the felt, generated, moved, and redirected sensation in a body that is alive.

What you un-do — the dismantling: stripping off the beliefs, the traumas, the training, the conditioning, the ideologies, the ideals, the embedded patterns that prevent the practices from doing what they are built to do.

Both at once is the actual practice. The deliverable, in plain language: a conscious cock, a wide-open relaxed pussy, two bodies that finally know what they are for.

What we publish

Articles on the body practices, the dismantling work, energy work specifically, and the cultural context — written for the educated adult reader who is dissatisfied with the resolution at which most existing content addresses the subject.

We do not certify teachers. We do not run workshops. We do not sell weekend retreats. We do not have a private community you can pay to join. We publish writing, and we publish books. That is the entire business.

Our editorial position

  • Body-grounded. If a claim can't be verified by a reader in their own body within a reasonable time, we don't make it.
  • Anti-cult. We are explicitly hostile to the patterns that turn tantric teachers into gurus and tantric students into followers. Our orientation is to make every practice maximally extractable from any source, including ours.
  • Plain language. We use Sanskrit only when no English equivalent exists. We name physiological mechanisms when they explain what the practice is doing.
  • Adult-respect. We assume the reader is a competent adult capable of handling clear information about sexuality, anatomy, and the body. We do not soften, hedge, or moralize.
  • Anti-mythology. Where a practice works, we explain why it works. Where the cultural story around a practice doesn't hold up, we say so.

The companion publication is The Naked Mind, an essay newsletter on consciousness, sexuality, and the inherited frameworks that get in the way of both.
