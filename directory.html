The Vetted Practitioner Directory

A curated directory of practitioners working in the territory this site writes about — tantra teachers, somatic guides, tantric massage practitioners, breathwork facilitators, ritual workers, erotic photographers, and the sex workers who serve this lineage with skill and integrity. Every listed practitioner has agreed to full transparency about their training, their lineage, and any cult or guru affiliations they came up through.

What "vetted" means

Every listed practitioner has provided, on the record:

  • Real name + credentials — no anonymized listings, no fabricated names
  • Training lineage — every teacher they trained with, dates, locations, length of training
  • Cult and guru affiliations — past or present — disclosed openly. Non-disclosure disqualifies. The reader decides what they are comfortable with.
  • Any abuse allegations against their primary teachers — with links to public coverage where it exists
  • Scope of practice — what they do, what they don't do, what they're not trained for
  • Pricing — no hidden tiers, no "ask in DMs"
  • Real client testimonials — verifiable, not fabricated
  • Code of conduct agreement — consent, boundaries, no proselytizing, no upselling beyond stated scope, no certification mills

The vetting is editorial, not auctioned. Practitioners who pay for premium placement still have to pass the vetting. Money does not buy past the gate.

What we don't list

  • Practitioners with active, unresolved abuse allegations of their own
  • Practitioners who refuse to disclose their training history
  • Practitioners actively running certification mills or weekend-conversion funnels
  • Practitioners whose primary affiliation is to a cult that is currently active and recruiting
  • Practitioners making unverifiable claims of healing, channeling, or supernatural ability
  • Practitioners working outside their stated scope

This is a tighter filter than any directory currently working in this field. That is the point.

For readers using the directory

Two things to keep in mind:

  1. The vetting is not a personal endorsement. A listed practitioner has met the directory's transparency standard. That doesn't mean they're the right fit for you. Read the full profile, including the lineage notes. Ask the practitioner questions. Trust your body's signal in the first session.
  2. If a practitioner violates the code of conduct, tell us. Contact us via the report link in any listing. We investigate every report. Practitioners who violate the code are removed from the directory and the removal is public.
